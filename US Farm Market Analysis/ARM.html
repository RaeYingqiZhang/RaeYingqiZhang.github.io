What is Association Rule Mining ?

"Association rule mining, at a basic level, involves the use of machine learning models to analyze data for patterns, or co-occurrences, in a database. It identifies frequent if-then associations, which themselves are the association rules.An association rule has two parts: an antecedent (if) and a consequent (then). An antecedent is an item found within the data. A consequent is an item found in combination with the antecedent. Association rules are created by searching data for frequent if-then patterns and using the criteria support and confidence to identify the most important relationships. Support is an indication of how frequently the items appear in the data. Confidence indicates the number of times the if-then statements are found true. A third metric, called lift, can be used to compare confidence with expected confidence, or how many times an if-then statement is expected to be found true.Association rules are calculated from itemsets, which are made up of two or more items. If rules are built from analyzing all the possible itemsets, there could be so many rules that the rules hold little meaning. With that, association rules are typically created from rules well-represented in data." (TechTarget.com)
 
When applying ARM to the twitter dataset using apriori method, the thresholds are set to be ​support=0.02, conf=0.5, maxlen=2. 397 rules returned.

• The top 15 rows for support:











Support is an indication of how frequently the two items appear in the data. For example, in the first row of {food} and {organic}. the support 0.17 means the frequency of both words appeared in a trasaction - a tweet.


• The top 15 rows for confidence:













Confidence indicates the number of times the if-then statements are found true. For example, in the first row of {himalayas} and {mukteshwar}, the confidence is calculated by having the support value 0.021 divided by the frequency of {himalayas} appeared in the whole dataset. It means that given the frequency of {himalayas}. the frequency of {mukteshwar} is 1. It also means with the confidence equals to 1, we can interpret that the words in rhs always show up in the tweets where contains the words in the acoording lhs column. 


• The top 15 rows for lift:













Lift can be used to compare confidence with expected confidence, or how many times an if-then statement is expected to be found true.Association rules are calculated from itemsets, which are made up of two or more items. If rules are built from analyzing all the possible itemsets, there could be so many rules that the rules hold little meaning. When lift equals to 1, the two items are independent with each other. When lift bigger than 1, the two items have postive correlation. For example, in the frist row, {foode} and {exchange} has positive correlation since their lift value is 42,83.

Insights
The word "organic", and "food" are the two under the spotlight. There are more words connected to "organic" than "food". The word "breakfast" links with "organic", which probably indicates that "people who value organic food also value their breakfast", or "people who like to have more organic food in their breakfast". Also, from "best" and "health" which connect to "organic", it's possible to assume people think organic food is healthy and is the best. The other cluster is generated from "healthy". Several words are qiote unique and their connections to "healthy" and "organic food" might be interesting to explore. Similarly, the words "india", "himalayas", "nirvana", "mukteshwar" are between "organic" and "food", which could be an interesting topic to explore deeper.

All three graphs have a lot of shared words and similar connections, but how they are connected and the meanings behind each graphs are not quite the same. As mentioned in the previous paragraph, three association rules have three different meanings. Every rule gives us an idea about the relationship between two target items, which can be exploited in further data sicence topic.
