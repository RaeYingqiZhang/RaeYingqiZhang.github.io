Conclusion
Compared to using naive Bayes to predict the label of record data, using naive Bayes to predict text data is not that very ideal even we use stemming and normalization techniques. It might also be my labels in my original data share lots of similarities so it's hard for naive Bayes to predict the likelihood probabilities. 

Analyzing from the five confusion matrix heatmaps, I found naive Bayes always fails in predicting label "Dairy" and label "Farms", which means I could assume there are some similar content in the news abstract containing keyword "Dairy" and those containing keyword "Farms".
